## First-Time Homebuyers

### Psychology Profile

**Who they are:** Ages 25-40, transitioning from renting to ownership. Mix of single professionals, couples, and young families. Income $60K-$150K. Research-obsessed but overwhelmed by unfamiliar process. This is likely their largest financial decision ever.

**Core pain points:**

- Process feels opaque and filled with unfamiliar jargon
- Fear of overpaying or making an expensive mistake
- Down payment feels impossible to accumulate
- Conflicting advice from parents, friends, and the internet
- Uncertainty about what they can actually afford
- FOMO vs. fear of rushing into the wrong home

**Emotional drivers:** Desire for stability and control over their space, wealth-building anxiety (feeling behind), pride in achieving homeownership milestone, nesting instinct.

**Decision style:** Research-heavy, peer-influenced, risk-averse, timeline-flexible but emotionally urgent.

### Content Strategy

**Tone:** Educational without condescension. Reassuring and empowering. Transparent about challenges.

**Content pillars:**

1. Demystification (40%) — Process walkthroughs, jargon busting, step-by-step guides
2. Financial empowerment (25%) — Down payment strategies, credit tips, assistance programs
3. Market intelligence (20%) — Local updates framed for buyers, affordability analysis
4. Confidence building (15%) — Success stories, mistake prevention, encouragement

### Key Topics

FHA loans, down payment assistance, pre-approval vs pre-qualification, PMI, closing costs, earnest money, inspection contingencies, first-time buyer grants, credit score requirements, debt-to-income ratio, starter home, mortgage payment breakdown, homebuyer education, gift funds, appraisal process

### Data Emphasis

Lead with entry-level price points (lower quartile, not median). Always translate prices into monthly payments. Highlight low down payment options (3%, 3.5%). Compare to rent costs. Frame affordability optimistically with realistic pathways.
